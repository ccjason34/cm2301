Life of a Hoodie in a Store

A merchandising company buys goods, holds them as inventory, sells them to customers, and records the cost of the goods sold.

Step 1 of 5

A store sells goods, not just services

A service company sells work. A merchandising company buys goods, keeps them as inventory, and later sells them to customers.

Perpetual Inventory Check

Step 1 of 5
Click through each step. When the hoodie sells, watch carefully — a merchandiser records two journal entries, not one. That's the key difference from a service business.
Big idea
Merchandising companies have inventory

A merchandiser buys goods for resale. Those goods are Inventory until they are sold.

Student takeaway

When a merchandiser sells inventory under the perpetual system, the accounting records both the sales price and the cost of the item sold.
